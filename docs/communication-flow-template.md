## Communication Cadence & Calendar

### Regular Communications

#### Daily
- **Standup Updates** (if applicable)
  - **Audience**: Core development team
  - **Format**: 15-minute meeting or async update
  - **Content**: Progress, blockers, plans for the day
  - **Owner**: Project Manager

#### Weekly
- **Project Status Update**
  - **Audience**: Project Sponsor, key stakeholders
  - **Format**: Written report (see template below)
  - **Content**: Progress, next steps, risks, decisions needed
  - **Owner**: Project Manager

- **Team Sync Meeting**
  - **Audience**: Core project team
  - **Format**: 30-60 minute meeting
  - **Content**: Progress review, planning, risk discussion
  - **Owner**: Project Manager

#### Bi-Weekly
- **Sprint Review/Demo** (if using Agile)
  - **Audience**: Team, Product Manager, interested stakeholders
  - **Format**: Live demo or recorded video
  - **Content**: Completed features, user feedback
  - **Owner**: Product Manager

#### Monthly
- **Stakeholder Update**
  - **Audience**: Broader stakeholder group
  - **Format**: Email summary or presentation
  - **Content**: Milestone progress, upcoming work, risks
  - **Owner**: Communications Lead

- **Executive Briefing**
  - **Audience**: Executive leadership
  - **Format**: Executive summary document or presentation
  - **Content**: Strategic alignment, ROI, major risks/decisions
  - **Owner**: Project Sponsor

### Milestone-Based Communications

#### Project Kickoff
- **Kickoff Announcement**: Broad communication introducing the project
- **Kickoff Meeting**: Align team on goals, roles, processes
- **Stakeholder Briefing**: Share charter, timeline, expectations

#### Major Milestones
- **Milestone Reached**: Celebrate accomplishments, share outcomes
- **Demo Session**: Showcase progress to stakeholders
- **Feedback Request**: Gather input for next phase

#### Pre-Release
- **Release Preview**: What's coming, when, and why
- **Readiness Review**: Status check with stakeholders
- **Training/Documentation**: Prepare support teams and users

#### Release
- **Release Announcement**: Notify users of new features/changes
- **Release Notes**: Detailed change log
- **Support Brief**: Known issues, FAQs, troubleshooting

#### Post-Release
- **Adoption Metrics**: Share usage data and feedback
- **Retrospective Summary**: Key learnings and improvements
- **Thank You Notes**: Recognize contributors

### Ad-Hoc/Trigger-Based Communications

- **Risk Escalation**: When new high-impact risks are identified
- **Critical Blocker**: When project timeline is at risk
- **Scope Change**: When significant changes are proposed
- **Incident Update**: During and after production incidents

## Communication Best Practices

### General Guidelines
- **Be Transparent**: Share both successes and challenges openly
- **Be Timely**: Communicate proactively, especially for issues
- **Be Concise**: Respect stakeholder time with clear, focused messages
- **Be Consistent**: Maintain regular cadences to build trust
- **Be Accessible**: Make it easy for stakeholders to ask questions

### For Written Communications
- Lead with key takeaways or action items
- Use bullet points and formatting for scannability
- Include links to detailed documentation for those who want more
- Specify owners and due dates for action items
- Proofread for clarity and professionalism

### For Meetings
- Send agendas in advance
- Start and end on time
- Document decisions and action items
- Share meeting notes promptly
- Record sessions when possible for those who can't attend

### For Sensitive or Complex Topics
- Consider whether written or verbal communication is more appropriate
- Provide context and background
- Be empathetic and solution-focused
- Follow up in writing to confirm understanding
- Allow time for questions and discussion

## Feedback & Continuous Improvement

### Gathering Feedback on Communications
- Periodic surveys on communication effectiveness
- Retrospective discussions on what worked/didn't work
- Direct feedback requests after major communications
- Monitor engagement metrics (email open rates, meeting attendance, etc.)

### Adjusting the Communication Plan
- Review and update this plan quarterly or after major milestones
- Adapt based on stakeholder feedback and project needs
- Document lessons learned for future projects
